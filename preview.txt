Delay Line Memory
A tube and filled with mercury. At one end put a speaker at the other end a microphone. When you pulse the speaker, it creates a pressure wave. This takes time to propagate to the other end of the tube, where it hits the microphone, converting it back into an electrical signal. And we can use this propagation delay to store data.

sequential or cyclic-access memory - You could only read one bit of data from a tube at any given instant and if you wanted to access a specific bit, like bit 112, you'd have to wait for it to come around in the loop.

magnetostrictive delay lines - an electromagnet twisted a long wire one way or the other to represent ones or zeros

magnetic core memory - Memory that stores binary data (0 or 1) in the orientation of magnetic charges in ferrite cores about one-sixeenth-inch in diameter.

Magnetic tape - a medium for magnetic recording, made of a thin, magnetizable coating on a long, narrow strip of plastic film

Magnetic Drum Memory -a metal cylinder coated in a magnetic material for recording data. The drum was rotated continuously, and positioned along its length were dozens of read and write heads. These would wait for the right spot to rotate underneath them to read or write a bit of data.

hard disk drive (HDD) -A direct access storage device used to store and retrieve data from rapidly rotating disks coated with magnetic material.
